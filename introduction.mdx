Axilio is a cloud of **real Android phones** — physical hardware on live carrier
networks — that your code can allocate and drive programmatically. You write
Python; Axilio hands you a phone and a driver that sees the screen and acts on it.

## Why phones

Agents are good at the web because the web has APIs and accessible HTML. The mobile
world is the opposite: the apps people live in — banking, rideshare, marketplaces,
social, delivery — ship **no public API**. For most of them, the *only* interface
is the screen of a real phone.

So that's the interface Axilio gives your agent: a real phone, driven the way a
person drives one — look at the screen, find the thing, tap it.

<Info>
  Emulators and rooted devices get flagged. Axilio uses genuine Android hardware
  on real carrier SIMs, so apps behave the way they do for a real user.
</Info>

## The mental model

Three pieces, in order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Client">
    `Client()` is your authenticated connection to Axilio. It reads your API key
    and exposes every resource — devices, runs, workflows, billing — as a typed
    method.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Session">
    `client.session("ANDROID")` allocates a phone, opens its control channel, and
    hands you a driver. When your code is done, the session releases the phone
    automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver">
    The `MobileDriver` is how you *do* things: find on-screen text or elements,
    tap, type, swipe, read the screen, and take screenshots.
  </Step>
</Steps>

```python
from axilio.platform import Client

client = Client()  # reads AXILIO_API_KEY from the environment

with client.session("ANDROID") as driver:
    driver.find_text("Settings").tap()
    driver.find(query="the search box").type_into("axilio")
    png = driver.screenshot()
```

## What makes it different

- **Real hardware** — genuine Android phones with authentic sensors, fingerprints,
  and OS behavior. No emulator tells.
- **Carrier networks** — real telco SIMs and production connectivity, with
  carrier-grade IPs on every plan.
- **Vision-native** — find elements with natural language (`find(query=...)`),
  backed by vision models, instead of brittle coordinate scripts.
- **Parallel execution** — concurrent, isolated sessions with automatic load
  balancing across the fleet.
- **Predictable pricing** — per-second billing of device time, with no data or
  proxy fees.
- **Replay & traces** — full session replay and per-step traces to debug agents in
  production.
