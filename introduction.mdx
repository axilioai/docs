Axilio is a cloud of **real Android phones** that your code can control. You write
a few lines of Python; Axilio hands you a phone and lets you see its screen and
tap, type, and swipe — exactly like a person using it.

## Why a real phone?

Most apps have no way for software to plug into them directly — no public API. The
only way to use them is through the screen, on a real phone. So that's what Axilio
gives your agent: a real phone, used the way a person uses one.

<Info>
  Every device is a real, physical Android phone on a real carrier network — the
  same hardware and connectivity your users have.
</Info>

## How it works

Three things, in order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Client — your connection to Axilio">
    Reads your API key and lets you reach everything: phones, runs, billing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Session — borrow a phone">
    Ask for a phone and Axilio gives you one. It's yours until you're done, then
    it goes back to the pool. You only pay for the time you hold it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Driver — control the phone">
    Find things on the screen, then tap, type, and swipe.
  </Step>
</Steps>

```python
from axilio.platform import Client

client = Client()  # reads your API key from the environment

with client.session("ANDROID") as driver:
    driver.find(query="the search box").type_into("axilio")
    photo = driver.screenshot()
```

## What makes it different

- **Real hardware** — real consumer Android phones, the same devices people use
  every day, not emulators or virtual devices.
- **Carrier networks** — real SIMs on real carrier networks, with genuine mobile
  connectivity on every plan.
- **Find things in plain English** — describe what you want (`find(query="the blue
  Continue button")`) instead of writing brittle coordinates.
- **Run many at once** — start lots of phones in parallel, each isolated.
- **Simple pricing** — pay by the second for the time you use a phone.
- **Replay** — watch a recording of everything your agent did.
