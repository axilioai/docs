`screenshot` takes a picture of the screen and hands it back as image data
(a PNG) — to save, log, or look at later.

```python
from axilio.platform import Client

client = Client()

with client.session("ANDROID") as driver:
    picture = driver.screenshot()
    with open("screen.png", "wb") as f:
        f.write(picture)
```

Because it's plain image data, you can hand it straight to anything that takes an
image — no temporary file needed.

## A picture, or the text?

- **`screenshot`** gives you a **picture**. Use it to save or show what the screen
  looked like.
- **[`observe`](/driver/read-the-screen)** gives you the **text and icons** on the
  screen. Use it to decide what to do next.

<Tip>
  Take a screenshot when something goes wrong — it's the fastest way to see what
  the phone actually looked like.
</Tip>
