The driver is how you control a phone. A [session](/quickstart) hands you one.
From there, you find something on the screen and act on it.

```python
from axilio.platform import Client

client = Client()

with client.session("ANDROID") as driver:
    driver.find(query="the Settings icon").tap()
```

## Two ways to find something

- **By its text** — [`find_text`](/driver/find-by-text) finds anything with a
  visible label, like a button or a menu item. Fast and exact.
- **By describing it** — [`find`](/driver/find-with-vision) finds anything you can
  describe in plain English, like "the search box" or "the heart icon." Use it
  when there's no text to match.

Both hand back the thing you found, ready to act on.

## Acting on it

```python
driver.find_text("Settings").tap()
driver.find(query="the search box").type_into("hello")
```

`tap`, `type_into`, `long_press`, and `swipe_to` all work on whatever you found.
You can also [tap an exact spot](/driver/actions) or
[press device buttons](/driver/hardware-keys) directly.
